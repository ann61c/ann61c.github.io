Data Sorting Time

The goal of this project was meant to conduct an experiment on both our fields of Computer Science and analyzing the data in a statistical way that we learned in class. We are trying to find out the statistical differences by measuring times of sorting a data set by using different sorting algorithms.

The response variable is sorting time, which is measured in units of seconds (s). There are two factors in our experiment, Factor 1 is Algorithms in different time complexity, and Factor 2 is data size. We choose three levels for Factor 1, including O(n\log(n)), O(kn), and O(n^2), and four levels for factor 2, including 128, 256, 512, and 1024 Kilobytes.

Lossy Image Compression

The DCT is a common transformation used in lossy compression tasks by applying a version of the two-dimensional Discrete Cosine Transform (DCT) to blocks of the image, then writing out an encoding of each of the resulting blocks. The DCT will let us look at the frequency components in each image block, making it possible to store information about how the shade is changing within a block, rather than just storing intensity values for individual pixels.
